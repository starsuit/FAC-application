Here are some of the things I like to make, and how they have contributed to my coding journey...

Little robots

Mostly they crash into things. I was given my first Raspberry Pi two years ago, and I've been messing about with wiring and soldering ever since. My "takeaway tub" rover is my proudest achievement, and I learned Python to write a program that helps it avoid obstacles (usually) and drive autonomously. I wrote some much more successful code for a virtual rover in a Udacity Robotics project.

Spreadsheets

Yes, really. I work in a school, and my job is to analyse data and figure out where students need the most attention and support. My favourite bit? Putting this information into little spreadsheet "apps" for busy teachers. This is actually where I began coding: I've been teaching myself VBA and Google Apps Script for a couple of years.

...gains?

Gains means putting on muscle and lifting heavier weights. Over the last few years weight training has become a big part of my life. I've been powerlifting for a couple of years, and when I moved to London in early 2018 I joined a CrossFit gym to make friends and join the general community. I'm currently working towards my first ever pull-up!
